How We Work

PolluLink is a system of air purifying bus stops that leverages data such as fine particulate pollution, wind speeds, temperature, humidity, and presence of people to provide an air filtration bubble for citizens waiting at bus stops.

Our system is only meant to sense fine particulates and presence of people, however, by leveraging other publically available data such as wind speeds, temperature, humidity, and barometric pressure, trends in this data overtime can be leveraged to not only sense but predict when conditions will cause air pollution levels will become drastically high and dangerous to the public.

Take, for example, the case of the Donora Death Fog of 1948 -- which occurred 37 miles away from Pittsburgh and sparked the creation of clean air laws. There atmospheric conditions mixed with the pollution to create a condition where dangerous gasses were trapped close to the ground, killing 20 and compromising the health of more than 6000. With PolluLink we not only look at the amount of PM2.5, but how other conditions in the system affect the health and safety for all.

How Our Users Benefit



Guide Transportation Data


By using the Port Authority Bus stops as the backbone of our system, we can fully map and bring air pollution detection and shelters to the larger Pittsburgh community

Using our app solution all types of people, not just travelers, can seek temporary refuge under a PolluLink bus stop



Inform City Planning


The city of Pittsburgh can further leverage this data when making decisions on when to release emissions, and where and whether to allow new manufacturers to enter into business in the Pittsburgh area



Advise Neighborhoods & Individuals


Local citizens can be empowered by knowing when to stay indoors and take extra precautions, as well as whether to move to certain neighborhoods based on their pre-existing conditions that may be exacerbated by certain pollutions
