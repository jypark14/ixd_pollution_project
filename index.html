Pittsburgh pm2.5 Levels

PM2.5 refers to particulate matter with diameter of less than 2.5 mm.
Since they are so small and light, PM 2.5 stay longer in the air than
heavier particles, which increases the chances of humans and animals
inhaling them into the bodies.
Studies have found a close link between exposure to fine particles and
premature death from heart and lung disease. Fine particles are also
known to trigger or worsen chronic disease such as asthma,
heart attack, bronchitis and other respiratory problems.

How PolluLink Works

PolluLink Offers The Following Features

Air Purification

When users are present within the bus stops, Pollulink provides an air filtration bubble that protects them from fine particulate air pollution.

PM 2.5

PolluLink senses PM 2.5 pollution data at all Pittsburgh bus stops which creates a network of pollution data for the individual citizens and larger municipalities.

City Planning

The data from PolluLink can be leveraged by the city of Pittsburgh and other large organizations for planning purposes such as informing decisions on when to release emissions.

Route Planning

The mobile app enables the users to plan different routes for trips on multiple factors such as distance, time, and pollution levels to allow users to avoid bus stops in areas with high pollution levels.

Mobile App

The mobile app provides a pollution dashboard, forecast information based off of predictive analytics run on the data we both collect and aggregate from external sources.

Data

Through partnerships with popular navigation solutions such as google maps and Transit, we can help inform Port Authority on the usage of their bus stops.
